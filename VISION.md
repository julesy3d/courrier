# The App — Vision & Direction

> Internal document. Brainstorming, positioning, and marketing reference.
> Last updated: 2026-04-11

---

## The problem

Remember the internet 10 years ago?

You'd open 9GAG and see the top pictures. The best stuff floated to the top because people voted on it. Simple. Transparent. Human.

Then someone decided to make it complicated. The ranked list disappeared. In its place: an algorithm. A black box that didn't show you the best content — it showed you the most *engaging* content. Not too entertaining, not too boring. Just the right amount of brain rot to keep you scrolling.

That algorithm spread everywhere. Instagram, TikTok, Facebook — all running the same play. A machine decides what you see, and its only goal is to keep you on the app for as long as possible.

The result? Nobody trusts what they're shown anymore. The best photo doesn't win. The most provocative one does. Creators don't make things for people — they make things for the algorithm.

**We lost the human in the loop.**

---

## What we're building

A daily photo contest, judged entirely by humans. No theme. No rules. Just one question: **do you think your picture is the best?**

Post what you want. Judge what you see. The best photo wins. Every day.

---

## The core experience

You open the app. Your entire screen is split in two: **two photos, stacked vertically, filling the display edge to edge.** No UI chrome, no usernames, no distractions. Just two images competing for your attention.

You swipe one away — or double-tap the one you prefer. The loser flies off the screen with a satisfying physics-based fling. The survivor stays. A new challenger takes the empty slot. Two photos again. You swipe again.

**This is not a feed.** You never see one post at a time. You always see two, head to head. Every swipe is a vote: "this one is better than that one." The app is a tournament happening in real time across every user's screen simultaneously.

The photos you're judging are random. There's no algorithm deciding which photo to show you next. No engagement optimization. Just a shuffled deck of today's submissions, dealt out in pairs.

Duel after duel, the community converges on a winner — not through likes or comments, but through thousands of individual binary choices: this one or that one.

At midnight, the leaderboard resets. New day. New contest. Anyone can win.

---

## The daily loop

| What | How |
|------|-----|
| Post | Take a photo or pick from your camera roll. Add a caption if you want. Submit it to today's arena. |
| Judge | Swipe through duels. Each swipe is a vote. The more you judge, the more the best photos rise. |
| Win | At midnight UTC, today's leaderboard freezes. The photo with the most wins is the winner. Then it resets. |

**One winner. Every day. Anyone can take it.**

---

## Why it works

**It's a new interaction model.** Nobody has done the two-at-a-time duel as a primary content interface. Every other app shows you one thing and asks "do you like this?" We show you two things and ask "which is better?" That's a fundamentally different — and more honest — question.

**It's fair.** Every photo enters the same arena. There's no boost for posting at the right time, no advantage for having followers, no way to game a feed. The only thing that matters is whether your photo survives more duels than everyone else's.

**It's open.** No themes, no constraints, no rules about what you can post. Share what you think is worth sharing. The crowd decides if you're right.

**It's finite.** There is a winner, and then it's over. No infinite scroll. No "one more video." The app has a natural stopping point every single day.

**It's human.** No recommendation engine. No machine learning model deciding what's "relevant" to you. Every single ranking decision is made by a real person swiping on their screen.

## Progression & recognition

The app rewards participation through **stickers and achievements** — collectible badges tied to real milestones:

- **Judging streaks** — Vote on duels for X consecutive days
- **Volume badges** — Judge 100, 500, 1000 duels
- **Winner stickers** — Your photo won the daily contest
- **Podium finishes** — Top 3, top 10, top 100
- **Consistency rewards** — Submit a photo every day for a week, a month

Stickers are visible on your profile. They're bragging rights, not currency. There's no marketplace, no unlockable features, no pay-to-win. You earn them by showing up and making good photos.

This is a social app — people compete, earn recognition, and build a track record over time. But the social layer is built on *merit* (your photos and your judgment), not on *graph* (who you follow).

## Who it's for

Young Europeans, 20-30. People who grew up on the good internet and can feel that something went wrong. People who are tired of being manipulated by feeds but still want a place to share and compete. Creative but not "creators." Competitive but not toxic.

The kind of person who has strong opinions about which photo is better — and wants a way to prove it.

---

## What it's not

- **Not an algorithmic feed.** No black box decides what you see. The content is shuffled randomly and judged by humans.
- **Not engagement-optimized.** The app has a natural end point every day. It wants you to check the winner and close it.
- **Not a follower game.** There are no follower counts, no influencer dynamics. A day-one user's photo has the same chance as anyone else's.

## Open questions

1. Should past winners be visible? A hall of fame creates aspiration but also an archive.
2. What does day 1 look like with 10 users vs. 10,000? The duel mechanic scales, but the "one winner" stakes change.
3. Is the European focus a brand choice or a growth constraint? Both can work, but the answer shapes the name and tone.
